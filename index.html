About Impact IQ

What is Impact IQ?

Impact IQ is an interactive simulation platform designed to help students and fresh professionals understand how modern corporate IT projects operate.

Many graduates enter their first job without fully understanding how Agile teams collaborate, manage requirements, or resolve workplace challenges.

Impact IQ allows users to step into different professional roles within a project team and experience realistic workplace situations.

Instead of learning only theoretical concepts, players experience Agile project environments where they must make decisions that affect project delivery, team collaboration, and stakeholder outcomes.

How the Simulation Works

Learn by stepping into the project, not just reading about it

01

Choose Your Role

Select a professional role such as Project Manager, Scrum Master, Business Analyst, HR Executive, Quality Analyst, or Developer.

Each role brings a different perspective, responsibility, and challenge.

02

Experience Agile Project Situations

Work through realistic corporate scenarios such as sprint planning, requirement clarification, stakeholder conflicts, team communication, and backlog prioritisation.

03

Make Decisions

Choose how to respond in each situation and observe how your actions influence trust, delivery, communication, and progress.

04

Reflect and Learn

After each scenario, receive feedback that explains how your decisions shaped the outcome and what that means in a real workplace context.

Why Cultural Awareness Matters

Projects are built by people, not robots with perfect communication

Modern project teams are often global, with members from different cultural and linguistic backgrounds. Communication styles, expectations, and decision approaches can vary across teams.

Impact IQ helps players recognise these differences and develop better ways to collaborate in diverse workplace environments.

Communication Styles

Not everyone speaks with the same level of directness or urgency.

Expectations

Different team members may interpret ownership, deadlines, and feedback differently.

Decision Approaches

Some prefer quick action. Others need alignment first. Real teams live in that tension.
